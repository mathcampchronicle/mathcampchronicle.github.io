5 things you didn’t know about our collective Slack usage

Unless you’ve already browsed the analytics page, in which case sorry for the false advertising.

Allison, week 1

The vmcsp-2021 Slack is a wonderful collection of important information, hilarious quotes and jokes, and parrots. Seriously, so many parrots. But what other interesting trends can one find in our usage of the platform? Well, counting messages by hand is kind of hard. Luckily for us, though, https://vmcsp-2021.slack.com/stats exists! Here’s a roundup of interesting information we can glean from this page.

1. The most files uploaded in one day (so far) was 291.

That’s right! On Saturday of week 1, July 10th, a whopping 291 files were uploaded to Slack. The day before had only 120, while the day after had a measly 33 (although that’s good to know, since it means people are really taking Sundays off). Why such a spike? Well, Saturday was the day of the LARP field trip, which probably played a major part in this trend. Will this record be beaten, by future field trips or other events? We’ll see!

2. The overwhelming majority of messages are sent in DMs.

Since camp began, between 68% and 82% of daily messages were sent in DMs. That’s huge! The overall amount of messages also followed an interesting trend, reaching its current max on Friday, July 9th (over 5000 messages, 70% of those were DMs!) and then dropping sharply on Sunday to only 1,325 messages (so little, wow).

3. #mysteries-v2 is the most active channel - yes, more than the main lounge!

Since its creation, #mysteries-v2 has had 694 messages posted, despite only including 27 members. That’s more than #main-lounge, which, despite including 158 members, only posted 639 messages in the last 30 days. Hats off to the 27 people hard at work unraveling the mysteries of Mathcampus; tell us in the main lounge about your accomplishments one day!

4. The main lounge has the most active members, though.

Eighty-eight different people have posted in the main lounge, with the closest runner-up - #introductions - having only 44 different posting members. The next biggest channels are #birthday-rock, #tech-support, and #quote-log, but each of them has had less than 35 users post a message.

5. Lizka and Phoebe are awesome at reacting to messages!

While I couldn’t sort by the “reactions added” column, I scrolled through it to see if there were any standouts - and boy, were there ever! Phoebe has reacted 1,589 times, with Lizka following closely behind at 1,524 reactions, as of this writing. What’s more, I couldn’t find a single other person to have cracked 1,000. What good staff!
